# Hackathon 2-Minute Demo

## Demo Goal
Win the room by proving three things fast:
- this is clearly a Track 3 sidekick, not an autonomous replacement
- the product works in a live-feeling moment instead of a static explanation
- the system is technically deeper than a single prompt because it senses hesitation, grounds assistance, and then gets out of the way

## Track 3 Rubric Translation
Your demo should visibly score against all four weighted criteria:
- `Adaptation & Personalization (35%)`: show that AndOne changes behavior based on this speaker's hesitation and recovery patterns, not generic prompting
- `Interaction Design (25%)`: show a UI that stays quiet, gives one compact cue, explains itself, and keeps the human in control
- `Contextual Awareness & Anticipation (25%)`: show that it helps before the moment fully collapses by combining transcript, live event, co-host state, and retrieval context
- `Ethics & Accessibility (15%)`: show consent, safe defaults, minimal intervention, transparency, and optional multimodal signals only when the user wants them

If a sentence in the demo does not support one of those four buckets, it is probably disposable.

## Core Story
AndOne is an adaptive sidekick for live commentary. It stays silent while the human is in rhythm, detects hesitation from multiple signals, surfaces one grounded cue at the right moment, and fades once confidence returns.

That is the whole demo spine. Everything you show should reinforce it.

The strongest framing sentence is:

`This is not AI commentary. This is adaptive confidence support for live performance.`

## Winning Demo Shape
Use this structure:
1. Show normal human commentary first.
2. Trigger a real hesitation moment.
3. Let AndOne step in with one compact cue.
4. Show recovery and weaning.
5. Trigger a different hesitation pattern, not just silence.
6. Show a different type of help.
7. Briefly reveal `Why this cue` and analytics/review to prove depth.
8. End on the system design and why it fits the sidekick track.

This is stronger than opening with architecture, because judges will first feel the value and only then understand the machinery.

## Recommended 2-Person Roles
- `Teammate A`: live commentator using the booth
- `Teammate B`: narrator/operator controlling the story and calling out what judges should notice

Best dynamic:
- A performs the live problem
- B translates the product behavior into judge language
- both speak with urgency and confidence
- avoid long handoffs or explaining menus

## Exact 2-Minute Screenplay

### 0:00-0:12 - Hook
`B: Live commentary is a craft. The worst AI demo here would be one that tries to replace the commentator. AndOne does the opposite. It adapts to the speaker, waits, senses hesitation, gives one grounded nudge, and disappears.`

Action:
- replay paused on the live booth
- mic enabled
- scoreboard and booth visible

### 0:12-0:32 - Show Silence While Human Is Strong
`B: Watch this first. We are live, but the system stays quiet while the speaker is in rhythm.`

`A:` deliver 1-2 strong natural lines over the opening sequence.

Suggested line:
`Barcelona are building nicely here, patient in possession, trying to pull Madrid out before that final ball arrives.`

`B: No card, no interruption, no chatbot. That is intentional.`

Action:
- press play
- let the opening action run

### 0:32-0:52 - Hesitation Trigger 1: Pause + Unfinished Thought
`A:` as the save sequence hits, intentionally hesitate:
`Lewandowski nearly got through there and that is a huge...`

Then pause.

`B: Right here AndOne detects more than a pause. It sees an unfinished phrase, a hesitation spike after a high-salience event, and that the co-host is no longer speaking. This is anticipation, not just reaction.`

Action:
- let the cue card appear naturally
- do not talk over it immediately

### 0:52-1:06 - Assist Appears, Human Recovers
`A:` pick up the cue naturally using your own words, not a robotic read:
`...huge stop from Courtois, and that may be the moment that keeps Madrid level.`

`B: One line. Grounded in the live moment. Not writing the whole broadcast, just getting the commentator back on track.`

Action:
- show the cue shrink/fade as confidence returns

### 1:06-1:24 - Hesitation Trigger 2: Filler / Restart
`A:` create a different failure mode on the counter:
`Madrid can break here, Vinicius is, um... Vinicius is...`

`B: Our hesitation system is not just dead air. It also watches fillers, repeated starts, pace pressure, wake phrases, and recovery. That is the personalization layer, because hesitation looks different for different people.`

Action:
- let second assist appear
- this proves the system handles more than silence

### 1:24-1:37 - Explainability Flash
`B:` click `Why this cue`

`B: And the assist is explainable. It is grounded in live match events, transcript context, social and vision cues when available, and the current hesitation state. The human can see why this appeared and still choose whether to use it.`

Action:
- keep this open for only a beat
- judges should see source chips and trace, not read paragraphs

### 1:37-1:50 - Review / Analytics Flash
`B:` switch briefly to the saved review or analytics surface

`B: After the session, AndOne does not just help in the moment. It turns the hesitation trace into coaching: peak hesitation, longest pause, prompt count, and an AI review of what went well and what to improve.`

Action:
- show saved trace + AI review panel quickly

### 1:50-2:00 - Close on Architecture and Track Fit
`B: Under the hood, this is a real sidekick system: hesitation sensing agents decide when to help, context agents keep a rolling world model fresh, cue and grounding agents generate one safe assist, and the review agent coaches you after. That is why this fits Track 3: adaptive, anticipatory, human-controlled AI.`

## Best Live Lines For Teammate A
These are easy to perform and map cleanly to product behavior.

### Strong opening line
`Barcelona are probing here, moving Madrid side to side and waiting for one opening in the final third.`

### Pause / unfinished-thought trigger
`Lewandowski nearly found the gap there and that is a massive...`

Then stop.

### Filler / restart trigger
`Madrid can break here, um... Vinicius is... Vinicius is already racing into space.`

### Recovery line after assist
`That save may change the whole rhythm of this sequence and suddenly Madrid have a way out.`

## Hesitation Triggers To Explicitly Mention
Say this once, cleanly, during the second trigger or closing architecture line:

`We define hesitation as a pattern, not just silence: pause duration, unfinished phrases, filler density, repeated openings, wake phrase requests, pace pressure, and whether recovery is happening.`

That sentence is important. It makes the system sound intentional and differentiated.

Optional follow-up if you have one more breath:

`Over time, those thresholds can become speaker-specific, so the system adapts to your style instead of forcing everyone into one definition of hesitation.`

## Ethics And Control Line
You should say one sentence that explicitly covers the ethics bucket:

`AndOne is opt-in, transparent, and human-controlled. It only listens for support cues, shows why it intervened, and optional health or visual signals would only exist with explicit consent.`

This should be said either during `Why this cue` or in the closing line.

## Optional Infrastructure Flex
If a judge seems technical and you have 5 extra seconds, use:

`This sports demo is one instantiation of a broader infrastructure layer. The same hesitation-and-recovery loop can support podcasts, interviews, pitches, hosts, educators, or accessibility use cases.`

That broadens the ambition without weakening the clarity of the sports demo.

## Best Closing For This Rubric
Prefer this over a generic ending:

`AndOne personalizes to how you hesitate, anticipates before the moment breaks, keeps the interface minimal, and leaves the human fully in control. That is exactly what a Sidekick should feel like.`

## What To Physically Click
- `Go live`
- `Play`
- `Why this cue`
- saved session / review tab only for a quick flash

Avoid clicking around the product beyond that. Every extra click costs trust.

## Judge-Facing Soundbites
Use these exact ideas throughout:
- `This is a sidekick, not an auto-commentator.`
- `It stays quiet while the human is good.`
- `It intervenes only when confidence drops.`
- `It gives one grounded line, not a wall of AI text.`
- `It backs off as soon as recovery starts.`
- `It is explainable in the moment and coachable after the moment.`

## What To Cut
Do not spend time on:
- setup steps
- long architecture diagrams
- every data source individually
- implementation details before the live moment
- generic claims like `we use agents`

Only mention the system depth after the judges have already seen it work.

## Backup Plan If Timing Slips
- If the natural hesitation window is missed, use `Force Hesitation`.
- If the cue appears slightly early, keep going and frame it as anticipation rather than interruption.
- If the review panel feels risky in the live run, skip it and keep the close focused on `Why this cue` plus the architecture sentence.

## Final Recommended Delivery Notes
- Teammate A should perform, not explain.
- Teammate B should explain, not overperform.
- Keep the tone premium and controlled, not frantic.
- Pause after the cue appears so judges can actually notice it.
- The fade/wean moment matters as much as the surfacing moment. Call it out.

## 20-Second Alternate Close
Use this if you are running out of time:

`AndOne helps exactly when a live commentator needs it and disappears when they do not. The hesitation system senses real breakdown patterns, the context system keeps the cue grounded, and the review system turns a live save into future improvement. That is an AI sidekick in the strongest sense of the word.`
